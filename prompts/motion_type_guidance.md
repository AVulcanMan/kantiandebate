## policy
Make each motion SPECIFIC and concrete. Name a specific ACTOR (e.g. 'The US Federal Government', 'The EU', 'This House') and a specific, well-defined ACTION or MECHANISM. Avoid vague verbs like 'improve', 'address', 'do more about', or 'support' — instead prescribe a concrete instrument (ban, mandate, tax, subsidize, decriminalize, nationalize, require, abolish, condition aid on, etc.) applied to a precise target. A debater should know exactly what world they are defending.

## value
Frame a clear normative or comparative-priority judgement with a definable criterion. Make the two values in tension explicit (e.g. liberty vs security, individual vs collective).

## fact
Make an empirical claim that is concretely measurable, naming the specific phenomenon and the outcome being assessed. Prefer 'X does more harm than good' / 'X is more effective than Y' framings.
